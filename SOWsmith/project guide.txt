SOWsmith — grounded, governed RFP/SOW drafting for Procurement
Problem
Procurement and vendor-management teams produce high-stakes bid packages (RFPs and SOWs) under tight timelines. Today, drafting is often built on copy/paste from prior work, which creates three recurring issues:

Inconsistency: structure, tone, and clauses vary across buyers and business units.
Risk: non-standard commercial/legal terms can slip through without the right review and audit trail.
Slow cycle time: generating a “ready-to-send” first draft can take days of back-and-forth.
At the same time, procurement faces rising compliance/audit pressure, increasing volume and complexity, and strong demand for AI-enabled speed with guardrails.

Target users
Primary users are Procurement / Vendor Management professionals (buyers, sourcing managers, category managers) who need to issue RFPs and standardize SOW/RFP packages across the organization, while ensuring legal and policy compliance.

Proposed solution
SOWsmith is a Microsoft 365 Copilot agent (built in Copilot Studio) that turns a short set of intake notes into a polished, house-style RFP or seven-section SOW in minutes—grounded in the organization’s own approved documents and routed through a mandatory human approval workflow.

Key capabilities:

House-style drafting: produces standardized RFP/SOW structure, language, and tone.
Grounded generation with citations: uses permission-aware retrieval over approved exemplars and clause libraries so drafts are based on real, organization-approved content (with citations) and avoid invented facts.
Non-standard term detection: identifies deviations from standard commercial/legal terms and clearly flags LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED.
Governed lifecycle: every draft is routed for human approval in Teams (and email if desired), with an audit trail.
How it works (end-to-end flow)
Intake: buyer selects RFP vs SOW and provides brief project notes (scope, timelines, vendor context).
Retrieve approved context: agent pulls relevant exemplars/clauses from the approved library using Foundry IQ (permission-aware, cited).
Draft package: generates a complete house-style RFP or SOW; carries domain terms verbatim and uses bracketed placeholders instead of inventing values.
Policy/risk checks: deterministically flags non-standard legal/commercial language and routes accordingly.
Create artifact: saves an editable Word document to SharePoint with routing metadata.
Human approval: sends a synchronized approval request to Microsoft Teams (and email) for Approve/Reject.
Audit + publish: moves the document to Approved/Rejected and writes an audit-log entry.
Demo scenarios (hackathon)
The demo will show procurement-focused, end-to-end scenarios:

Creating a new RFP from a short intake → drafted RFP → deviations flagged → Legal approval in Teams → approved package saved to SharePoint with audit trail.
Drafting a vendor SOW from approved exemplars → seven-section SOW with citations/placeholders → approval workflow → finalized Word output + audit log.
Updating an existing template → highlights deltas vs standard language → triggers Legal review only when deviations exist → publish with traceability.
Reliability, safety, and governance
Grounding + permissions: drafts are based on approved internal content and respect access controls.
No invented numbers: uses placeholders for unknown values rather than fabricating details.
Deterministic guardrails: structure, legal routing, validation, and audit logging are rule-driven; the model is used for prose only.
Human-in-the-loop by default: nothing is issued without explicit approval.
Expected impact
Faster cycle time: first drafts in minutes/hours instead of days.
Higher consistency: standardized structure and language across procurement teams.
Reduced risk: reliable deviation detection, enforced legal review, and auditability.
Tech stack
Microsoft Copilot Studio · Azure AI Foundry (Foundry IQ) · Azure OpenAI (GPT-4o) · SharePoint Online · Power Automate (Approvals) · Microsoft Teams · (Optional) Python reference engine + automated tests mirroring platform logic.
